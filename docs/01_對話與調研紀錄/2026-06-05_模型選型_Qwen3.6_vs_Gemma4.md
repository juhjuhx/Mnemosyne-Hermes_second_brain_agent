# 2026-06-05 模型選型 — Qwen 3.6 vs Gemma 4

> Why we picked `Qwen 3.6 27B-PRISM-DQ` for workstation and `Gemma 4 E2B` for M1.
> Decision rationale, alternatives considered, and rollback plans.

---

## TL;DR

| Machine | Picked model | Size | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| M1 (8GB) | `unsloth/gemma-4-e2b-it-GGUF` Q4_K_XL | ~2.5GB | Best 2B-class quality in 2026; runs on Metal; M1 RAM-safe |
| Workstation (A770 16GB) | `Ex0bit/Qwen3.6-27B-PRISM-PRO-DQ` Q4 | ~13.7GB | Best 27B-class with bundled MTP speculative decoding |
| Workstation (backup) | `mudler/Carnice-Qwen3.6-MoE-35B-A3B-APEX-MTP-GGUF` I-Compact | ~17GB | Slightly over budget; kept for swap-in after benchmark |

## 1. M1 (8GB) — Gemma 4 E2B

### Candidates considered

| Model | Size (Q4) | M1 fit | Quality | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| `unsloth/gemma-4-e2b-it-GGUF` Q4_K_XL | 2.5GB | ✅ yes | Strong (Gemma 4 release) | **Picked** |
| `Qwen 3.6 1.7B GGUF` | 1.5GB | ✅ yes | Decent | Backup if Gemma fails |
| `Llama 3.3 3B GGUF` | 2.2GB | ✅ yes | Decent | Older; deprioritized |
| `Phi-4 mini 3.8B GGUF` | 2.8GB | ⚠️ tight | Good | Slightly over budget |
| `Llama 3.1 8B GGUF` | 5.0GB | ❌ no | Good | Doesn't fit with macOS+Hermes |

### Why Gemma 4 E2B wins

1. **2.5GB is the sweet spot** — leaves ~1GB headroom on M1's 8GB.
2. **Gemma 4 (2026)** is the latest Google release; quality is strong for 2B class.
3. **GGUF + Ollama + Metal** is the lowest-friction path on M1.
4. **Unsloth's quant** is well-tested, no quality surprises.
5. **Hermes Agent compatibility** — Gemma models are in Hermes's tested set.

### Rollback

If Gemma 4 E2B underperforms on eval:
- First swap: `Qwen 3.6 1.7B GGUF` (smaller, faster)
- Second swap: `Phi-4 mini 3.8B GGUF` (tighter RAM)
- Last resort: Drop Ollama from M1 entirely; route everything to workstation's Qwen.

## 2. Workstation (A770 16GB) — Qwen 3.6 27B-PRISM-DQ

### Candidates considered

| Model | Size (Q4) | A770 16GB fit | Quality | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| `Ex0bit/Qwen3.6-27B-PRISM-PRO-DQ` | 13.7GB | ✅ yes (2.3GB spare) | Strong + MTP | **Picked** |
| `Qwen 3.6 32B GGUF` Q4 | 19GB | ❌ no | Strong | Doesn't fit |
| `Qwen 3.6 14B GGUF` Q4 | 9GB | ✅ yes | Decent | Smaller; for benchmarking |
| `mudler/Carnice-Qwen3.6-MoE-35B-A3B-APEX-MTP-GGUF` I-Compact | 17GB | ⚠️ tight | Strongest (35B MoE) | Backup |
| `Llama 3.3 70B GGUF` Q4 | 42GB | ❌ no | Strong | Way over budget |
| `DeepSeek V3.2 37B GGUF` Q4 | 22GB | ❌ no | Strong | Over budget |

### Why Qwen 3.6 27B-PRISM-DQ wins

1. **Fits A770 16GB with 2.3GB to spare for KV cache.**
2. **Bundled MTP head** = self-speculative decoding in a single file (no separate draft model).
3. **80 tok/s → 121 tok/s** with MTP enabled (per Ex0bit benchmark).
4. **Qwen 3.6 (2026-04-16)** is the latest Alibaba release.
5. **Apache 2.0** license — fully commercial-friendly.
6. **Hermes Agent compatibility** — Qwen models are Hermes-tested.

### What is "PRISM" and "DQ"?

- **PRISM** = Ex0bit's quant method that bundles a draft head (MTP) into the model
  for self-speculative decoding. The model uses its own earlier layers to predict
  multiple tokens ahead, then verifies them in one forward pass.
- **DQ** = Dynamic Quantization. Some layers are kept at higher precision (e.g. Q6)
  while others are aggressively quantized (e.g. Q3). Per-layer, not per-model.
- Result: smaller file than naive Q4 + faster inference than naive Q3.

### How to enable MTP in llama-server

```bash
llama-server \
  --model Ex0bit/Qwen3.6-27B-PRISM-PRO-DQ.Q4_K_M.gguf \
  --speculative-model-draft auto \  # uses bundled MTP head
  --draft-max 8                       # tokens drafted per step
  --gpu-layers 99 \
  --ctx-size 32768 \
  --port 8642 \
  --host 127.0.0.1
```

(In llama.cpp b4567+, the `--speculative-model-draft auto` flag enables the
MTP head that's stored in the PRISM-DQ .gguf's metadata.)

### Rollback

If 27B PRISM underperforms:
- First swap: `mudler/Carnice-Qwen3.6-MoE-35B-A3B-APEX-MTP-GGUF` I-Compact 17GB
  (slightly over VRAM budget; will need to set `--ctx-size 16384` and offload
  2-3 layers to system RAM via `--no-kv-offload`).
- Second swap: drop to `Qwen 3.6 14B GGUF` Q4 (much faster, less quality).
- Last resort: `Qwen 3.6 7B GGUF` Q6 (very fast, modest quality).
